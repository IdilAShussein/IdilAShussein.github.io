5 Days of Around the World

Japanese Strawberry Shortcake

A delightful fluffy cake with fresh strawberries and cream.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cake flour
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 lb fresh strawberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix flour and sugar. Add milk and butter. Beat until smooth.
  3. Pour batter into cake pans and bake for 25-30 minutes.
  4. Whip cream with vanilla. Slice strawberries.
  5. Layer cake with whipped cream and strawberries.

2. French Macarons

Colorful, delicate, and delicious French almond-based cookies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Sift powdered sugar and almond flour.
  3. Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gradually add granulated sugar.
  4. Fold in the almond mixture.
  5. Pipe onto baking sheets and bake for 15 minutes.
  6. Prepare filling by mixing butter, powdered sugar, and cream.

3. Mochi Ice Cream (Japan)

A Japanese rice cakes with an ice cream center in a soft, slightly chewy, decadent rice dough.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sweet rice flour (mochiko)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup water
  • Cornstarch for dusting
  • Ice cream (any flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Mix sweet rice flour, sugar, and water in a microwave-safe bowl.
  2. Cover and microwave for 1 minute, then stir. Repeat until the dough is translucent.
  3. Dust a surface with cornstarch and roll out the dough.
  4. Cut circles and place a small scoop of ice cream in the center. Wrap and freeze.

4. Gulab Jamun (India)

A classic Indian sweet made with milk solids, sugar, rose water & cardamom powder.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder

Instructions:

  1. Mix milk powder, flour, and baking soda. Add butter and milk to form a dough. Roll into small balls.
  2. Fry the balls until golden brown.
  3. Simmer sugar, water, and cardamom powder to make syrup. Soak fried balls in syrup before serving.

5. Russian Pavlova

A meringue-based cake made of whipped egg whites and sugar giving a light, airy marshmallow feeling.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup caster sugar
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream and mixed berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 250°F (120°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Beat egg whites until soft peaks form, then gradually add sugar until stiff and glossy.
  3. Gently fold in vinegar, cornstarch, and vanilla extract.
  4. Spread the meringue into a circle on the parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 1 hour, then turn off the oven and let the pavlova cool completely.
  6. Top with whipped cream and mixed berries before serving.
